💊 Drug Release Simulation

Modeling how medicine moves — visualizing zero-order and first-order drug delivery.

“Medications don’t just work because of what they are — they work because of how they’re delivered. That’s what this project taught me.”

🧪 Project Purpose

I wanted to better understand drug release kinetics — how medication is released from a pill, patch, or injection over time. This simulation compares two common delivery types:

  • Zero-Order Release: releases drug at a constant rate.
  • First-Order Release: rate depends on how much drug is left.

Using these models, biomedical engineers can control dosage, manage pain, and optimize treatment for chronic illnesses.

📊 Release Curve Visualization

I used Python and Matplotlib to simulate a 100 mg dose over time. The graph below shows how each model behaves. Zero-order gives a flat, steady line. First-order decreases exponentially.

💡 What I Learned

This project gave me a stronger understanding of patient-centered drug design. I learned how controlled release helps reduce side effects and keeps blood levels stable — crucial in pain management, hormone therapy, and cancer treatments. It helped me connect theory with real patient impact.
